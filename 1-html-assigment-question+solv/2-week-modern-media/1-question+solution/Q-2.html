HTML entities are special character codes used in HTML to display characters that are reserved or have special meaning in HTML. They are represented by an ampersand (&) followed by a unique entity name or a numerical code, and end with a semicolon (;). Here are five commonly used HTML entities:

    < (less than): Represents the less than symbol (<). It is used to display the less than sign without triggering the interpretation of an opening HTML tag.
    Example: 
This is <strong>important</strong>


    > (greater than): Represents the greater than symbol (>). It is used to display the greater than sign without triggering the interpretation of a closing HTML tag.
    Example: 
2 > 1


    & (ampersand): Represents the ampersand symbol (&). It is used to display the ampersand without starting an entity or character reference.
    Example: 
This & that


    " (quotation mark): Represents the quotation mark ("). It is used to display double quotation marks within a string.
    Example: 
"Hello, world!"


    © (copyright): Represents the copyright symbol (©). It is used to display the copyright symbol.
    Example: 
© 2023 My Company


These entities help ensure that special characters are displayed correctly in HTML, regardless of their reserved or special meaning.
