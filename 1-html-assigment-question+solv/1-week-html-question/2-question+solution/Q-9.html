Most Common Text Formatting Tags in HTML

  1. bold (b)
    • Use to make text bold and emphasize importance.
    • Best practice: Reserve for important content, headings, or important keywords.
  2. italic (i)
    • Use to emphasize or provide emphasis to specific text.
    • Best practice: Avoid using italics for long paragraphs, use sparingly for short phrases.
  3. underline (u)
    • Use to underline text for stylistic purposes or to indicate links.
    • Best practice: Avoid using underlines for non-link text, as it may confuse users.
  4. strikethrough (del)
    • Use to show deleted or outdated text.
    • Best practice: Use sparingly and with caution, as it can make text harder to read.
  5. superscript (sup)
    • Use to place text above the baseline, often for footnotes or mathematical symbols.
    • Best practice: Ensure the superscript doesn't disrupt the flow of the text.
  6. subscript (sub)
    • Use to place text below the baseline, often for chemical formulas or mathematical equations.
    • Best practice: Ensure the subscript doesn't disrupt the flow of the text.
